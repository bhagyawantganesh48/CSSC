Cyber Ethics & Laws

Understanding the ethical boundaries, legal frameworks, and responsible practices in cybersecurity.

CYBER ETHICS

The Ethical Framework

Ethics in cybersecurity defines the boundaries between legitimate security research and criminal activity.

✅

Permitted Activities

  • Penetration testing with written authorization
  • Security research on owned/controlled systems
  • Participation in authorized bug bounty programs
  • CTF competitions and sandboxed labs
  • Vulnerability research for academic purposes
  • Educating others about security awareness
❌

Prohibited Activities

  • Unauthorized access to any system or network
  • Scanning or probing systems without permission
  • Deploying malware or unauthorized tools
  • Data theft, eavesdropping, or interception
  • Sharing exploits for malicious purposes
  • Using club resources for illegal activities

Core Ethical Principles

01
Confidentiality

Never disclose sensitive information obtained during security assessments without authorization.

02
Integrity

Do not alter, damage, or destroy data or systems unless specifically required by the authorized scope.

03
Authorization

Always obtain written permission before testing any system, network, or application.

04
Reporting

Report all discovered vulnerabilities to the appropriate authority promptly and responsibly.

RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE

Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

🔍

Step 1: Discovery

Document the vulnerability thoroughly including affected systems, reproduction steps, and potential impact. Never exploit beyond what is necessary to confirm the issue.

📧

Step 2: Private Notification

Contact the organization's security team privately via their security email, bug bounty platform, or responsible disclosure policy page. Do NOT post on public forums.

⏰

Step 3: Wait Period (90 Days)

Allow the organization 90 days to patch the vulnerability before any public disclosure. Maintain strict confidentiality during this period.

🤝

Step 4: Coordinate Disclosure

Work with the organization to coordinate a joint disclosure once the fix is deployed. Credit may be given to the researcher in the vendor's security advisory.

📢

Step 5: Public Disclosure

After the patch is released, you may publish a technical writeup that educates the community without enabling exploitation of unpatched systems.

CYBER LAW

India's IT Act 2000

Key provisions of the Information Technology Act, 2000 that every cybersecurity practitioner must know.

§ Section 43

Unauthorized Access & Damage

Penalizes unauthorized access, downloading data, introducing viruses, or disrupting computer systems. Civil liability up to ₹1 crore for damages.

⚠️ Civil liability up to ₹1 Crore
§ Section 66

Computer Related Offences

Criminal version of Section 43. Dishonestly or fraudulently performing acts described in Section 43 is punishable with imprisonment and/or fine.

⚠️ Up to 3 years imprisonment + ₹5 Lakh fine
§ Section 66C

Identity Theft

Fraudulent use of electronic signature, password, or unique identification feature of another person for unauthorized access or benefit.

⚠️ Up to 3 years imprisonment + ₹1 Lakh fine
§ Section 66E

Privacy Violation

Intentionally capturing, publishing, or transmitting private images of another person without consent, violating their privacy.

⚠️ Up to 3 years imprisonment + ₹2 Lakh fine
§ Section 67

Obscene Content

Publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form is a criminal offence with severe penalties.

⚠️ Up to 3 years imprisonment + ₹5 Lakh fine
§ Section 70

Protected Systems

Unauthorized access or attempts to access computer resources designated as 'protected systems' by the government is a severe offence.

⚠️ Up to 10 years imprisonment

ETHICAL HACKING

Hacker's Code of Conduct

📋

Get Written Permission

Always obtain explicit written authorization before testing any system. Verbal permission is insufficient. Keep a signed authorization document at all times.

🎯

Define Clear Scope

Establish and strictly adhere to the defined scope of testing. Do not test systems, networks, or features not explicitly included in the authorization.

📝

Document Everything

Keep detailed records of all activities, findings, timestamps, and tools used. Proper documentation is essential for reporting and legal protection.

🔒

Protect Sensitive Data

Handle any data encountered during testing with extreme care. Immediately report and secure any sensitive data discovered; never retain it beyond what's needed.

🚨

Report Critical Findings Immediately

If you discover a critical vulnerability that poses immediate risk, notify the client immediately before completing the full assessment.

🛑

Stop When Instructed

Cease all testing activities immediately when requested by the system owner, even if work is incomplete. Always respect the client's decision to terminate testing.

DIGITAL RESPONSIBILITY

Being a Responsible Netizen

🔑

Use Strong Authentication

Enable MFA/2FA everywhere possible. Use password managers and unique, complex passwords for every account.

🔄

Keep Systems Updated

Regularly update operating systems, applications, and firmware. Unpatched systems are the primary vector for cyberattacks.

🧠

Think Before You Click

Be skeptical of unsolicited emails, links, and downloads. Phishing remains the most common attack vector worldwide.

🌐

Use Secure Networks

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities. Use VPN when necessary. Understand the risks of unencrypted connections.

💾

Backup Your Data

Follow the 3-2-1 backup rule: 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite. Protect against ransomware and hardware failures.

🗣️

Spread Awareness

Share cybersecurity knowledge with friends, family, and colleagues. A secure community starts with educated individuals.
