Policies & Legal Compliance

All club policies governing membership, conduct, legal obligations, and digital responsibility.

📜

These policies apply to all CSSC members, participants, and anyone engaging with club resources or events. By joining CSSC, you agree to abide by all policies listed below. Please read each section carefully.

Last Updated: February 2026  |  Version 2.0

1.1 Membership Terms

Membership in CSSC is open to all currently enrolled students of the university. Membership is granted upon successful registration, agreement to these terms, and payment of any applicable membership fees.

1.2 Use of Club Resources

Club resources including lab equipment, software licenses, network access, and digital tools are provided exclusively for educational, research, and club-authorized activities. Any misuse of resources may result in immediate membership suspension.

1.3 Participation Requirements

Members are expected to maintain active participation in club activities. Inactive members (no participation for 3+ months without prior notice) may be moved to associate membership status. Members must attend at least 60% of club events per semester.

1.4 Intellectual Property

Content, tools, or research developed during CSSC activities may be published under open-source licenses as determined by the club leadership. Members retain credit for their contributions.

1.5 Termination of Membership

CSSC reserves the right to suspend or terminate membership for violation of any policies, unethical behavior, or illegal activities. Appeals may be made to the faculty mentor within 7 days of suspension.

1.6 Amendments

These terms may be updated periodically. Members will be notified of significant changes via official communication channels. Continued membership after notification constitutes acceptance of amended terms.

📜 CSSC Member Oath

"I, as a member of the Cyber Security Students Club, solemnly declare that I will use the knowledge and skills gained through this club only for ethical, legal, and constructive purposes. I will not engage in any unauthorized access, data theft, network intrusion, or any activity that violates the law or compromises the privacy of others. I commit to upholding the principles of responsible disclosure, digital integrity, and cyber ethics in all my activities, both within and outside the club. I understand that violation of this oath may result in immediate expulsion from the club and potential legal consequences."

— Signed upon registration by all CSSC members

Declaration Points

  • I declare that all information provided during registration is accurate and truthful.
  • I understand that I am responsible for any activities conducted using my club credentials.
  • I agree to immediately report any security incidents or violations to club leadership.
  • I declare that I have read and understood all club policies and agree to comply with them.
  • I understand that my membership may be revoked for policy violations without refund of fees.

3.1 Professional Conduct

All members shall conduct themselves with professionalism and integrity in all club activities, events, and communications. Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind will not be tolerated.

3.2 Technical Integrity

Members shall not engage in unauthorized hacking, cracking, or exploitation of systems. All technical activities must be conducted in approved lab environments, with explicit permission, or on personally owned systems.

3.3 Knowledge Sharing

Members are encouraged to share knowledge but must exercise judgment regarding the appropriateness of sharing sensitive technical details (e.g., zero-day exploits, unpatched vulnerabilities).

3.4 Respect for Privacy

Members must respect the privacy and confidentiality of all individuals and organizations. Personal data encountered during any security activity must be protected and not disclosed or retained unnecessarily.

3.5 Conflict of Interest

Members must disclose any potential conflicts of interest in club activities, especially in competitions, evaluations, or external partnerships.

3.6 Disciplinary Process

Violations of the Code of Ethics will be reviewed by the ethics committee. Actions range from formal warnings to permanent expulsion based on severity. All decisions are documented and final after appeal review.

4.1 Official Club Accounts

Only designated social media managers may post from official CSSC social media accounts. Unauthorized use of official accounts will result in immediate membership action.

4.2 Personal Posting Guidelines

When posting about CSSC activities on personal accounts, members must:

  • Be respectful and accurate in all representations of the club
  • Not share confidential club information, member data, or private discussions
  • Clearly distinguish personal opinions from official club positions
  • Not post content that could embarrass the club, university, or other members
  • Obtain consent before tagging other members in club-related posts

4.3 Technical Content Sharing

Members must not share exploit code, malware samples, or sensitive security research on public platforms without proper disclosure completion and club leadership approval.

4.4 Prohibited Content

Members may not post content that is defamatory, obscene, discriminatory, or that violates intellectual property rights while representing CSSC.

5.1 Applicable Laws

All members must comply with applicable Indian and international laws, including but not limited to:

  • Information Technology Act, 2000 and IT (Amendment) Act, 2008
  • Indian Penal Code (relevant cyber crime sections)
  • Personal Data Protection Bill (India)
  • Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (for US-hosted systems)
  • GDPR (for European user data)
  • University IT usage policy

5.2 Data Protection

Member personal data collected during registration is stored securely and used only for club administration purposes. Data will not be shared with third parties without explicit consent, except as required by law.

5.3 Liability

CSSC and the university are not liable for any unauthorized or illegal activities conducted by individual members. Each member is personally and legally responsible for their own actions.

5.4 Law Enforcement Cooperation

CSSC will cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations involving club members or resources. Member anonymity will not be protected in cases of criminal conduct.

⚠️ Important Risk Disclosure

Cybersecurity activities inherently involve risks. By joining CSSC, members acknowledge the following:

6.1 Technical Risks

Security testing activities carry risks of unintended system damage, data loss, or service disruption even in authorized environments. Members must exercise caution and follow all safety protocols.

6.2 Legal Risks

Members acknowledge that performing security testing without proper authorization is illegal and may result in criminal prosecution. CSSC provides no legal protection for unauthorized activities conducted by members.

6.3 Reputational Risks

Members understand that unethical behavior or policy violations may result in academic consequences, including notation on university records, in addition to club disciplinary action.

6.4 Physical Lab Risks

Members using physical lab equipment acknowledge responsibility for proper handling. Damage due to negligence may result in financial liability to the member.

6.5 Member Acknowledgement

By registering as a CSSC member, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and accept all risks described in this section. You confirm that you will seek guidance from club leadership before attempting any activities that may carry legal or technical risk.

For questions about any policy, contact the CSSC leadership team at cssc@university.edu or speak with our Faculty Mentor.
